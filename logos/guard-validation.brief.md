# @hex-di/guard-validation

> See [LOGO-BRIEF.md](./LOGO-BRIEF.md) for shared brand context, color family, and technical specs.

## What It Is

GxP validation protocols: IQ (Installation Qualification), OQ (Operational Qualification), PQ (Performance Qualification), traceability matrices, compliance report generation. For pharma, biotech, medical device regulated environments.

## Role & Metaphor

**The Compliance Stamp.** The official certification seal that transforms code validation into regulatory documentation. Like a notary's stamp that says "this system has been formally verified per regulatory requirements."

## Key Concepts to Convey

- Formal validation (IQ/OQ/PQ phases)
- Regulatory compliance (official, certified)
- Traceability: requirements linked to evidence
- Documentation/certification seal

## Visual Direction

- A hexagonal seal/stamp shape - double-outlined hexagon (inner + outer ring)
- Inside: a checkmark with a horizontal line underneath (certified/validated signature)
- Or: a hexagon with a ribbon/badge element
- Should feel official, formal, authoritative

## Signature Color

`#7C3AED` (violet) - authority, certification, governance

## Designer Prompt

> Design a minimal geometric logo icon (200x200 SVG) of a double-outlined hexagon (inner and outer hexagon outlines, violet #7C3AED, outer stroke 4px, inner stroke 2px, ~15px gap between them) forming a seal/stamp shape. Inside the inner hexagon, place a bold checkmark above a thin horizontal line (like a signature line on a certificate). All elements in violet. Conveys: "formal regulatory validation seal for GxP compliance." No text. Dark background friendly.
