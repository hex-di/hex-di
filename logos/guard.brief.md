# @hex-di/guard

> See [LOGO-BRIEF.md](./LOGO-BRIEF.md) for shared brand context, color family, and technical specs.

## What It Is

Authorization framework: compile-time-safe permissions, hierarchical role DAG, ABAC policy engine, guard adapters that enforce access control at service boundaries, GxP-compliant immutable audit trails.

## Role & Metaphor

**The Fortress Shield.** Guard stands at the boundary of every protected service. It checks credentials, evaluates policies, records decisions, and either allows or denies entry. Part bouncer, part bureaucrat, part shield.

## Key Concepts to Convey

- Protection: shielding services from unauthorized access
- Policy evaluation: rules and decision-making
- Audit trail: recording what happened
- Gateway: guarding the entrance to the hexagon

## Visual Direction

- A hexagon with a shield or lock motif overlaid
- Or: a hexagon where the top half is a shield shape
- Could incorporate a keyhole or checkmark inside the shield
- Should feel authoritative and protective, not decorative

## Signature Color

`#DC2626` (red) - security, protection, alertness, authority

## Designer Prompt

> Design a minimal geometric logo icon (200x200 SVG) of a shield shape whose lower half merges into a hexagonal point (instead of a traditional shield's rounded bottom, it comes to a hexagonal vertex). Inside the shield-hexagon, place a bold keyhole symbol formed by a small circle above a narrow vertical rectangle. All elements in red (#DC2626). Stroke-based design (outline shield, stroke 6px; keyhole as negative space or lighter stroke). Conveys: "authorization shield guarding a hexagonal service boundary." No text. Dark background friendly.
