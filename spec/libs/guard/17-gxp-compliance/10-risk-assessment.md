## 68. Risk Assessment (FMEA)

This section provides a Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) for `@hex-di/guard` per ICH Q9 and GAMP 5 risk management guidance.

### Scoring Methodology

| Factor                | Scale | Description                                                                                        |
| --------------------- | ----- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Severity (S)**      | 1-5   | 1 = Negligible, 2 = Minor, 3 = Moderate, 4 = Major, 5 = Critical (patient safety / data integrity) |
| **Likelihood (L)**    | 1-5   | 1 = Remote, 2 = Unlikely, 3 = Possible, 4 = Likely, 5 = Frequent                                   |
| **Detectability (D)** | 1-5   | 1 = Immediate/automatic, 2 = Easy, 3 = Moderate, 4 = Difficult, 5 = Undetectable                   |

**Risk Priority Number (RPN)** = S x L x D. Maximum = 125.

```
REQUIREMENT: All failure modes with RPN >= 15 MUST have mitigations that bring
             the residual RPN to < 10. Failure modes with Critical severity
             (S=5) MUST additionally demonstrate that no single control failure
             can restore the pre-mitigation RPN.
```

> **FM-20 rate limiting note:** When `gxp: true`, `maxEvaluationsPerSecond` is REQUIRED on `createGuardGraph()`, so rate limiting is always active in GxP environments. The `RateLimitSummaryAuditEntry` ensures rate-limited evaluations are visible in the audit trail (ALCOA+ Complete). In non-GxP environments, rate limiting remains optional; organizations that do not implement rate limiting SHOULD document this in their risk assessment. The mitigated RPN for GxP mode is 3 × 1 × 1 = 3 (Detectability = 1 due to mandatory audit trail summary entries and automated alerting).

> **Non-GxP Residual RPN column:** The "Non-GxP Residual RPN" column reflects the residual risk when GxP-specific controls (those gated behind `gxp: true`) are not active. For most failure modes, the non-GxP residual matches the GxP mitigated RPN because the primary mitigations are not GxP-gated. Key differences: FM-07 (10 vs 5) — HSM is REQUIRED only when gxp:true, so non-GxP deployments rely on behavioral contract + IQ-10 only (D=2 instead of D=1); FM-09 (12 vs 8) — NTP monitoring is REQUIRED only when gxp:true, so non-GxP D=3 (RECOMMENDED monitoring); FM-19 (16 vs 8) — maxScopeLifetimeMs is REQUIRED only when gxp:true, so non-GxP retains L=2 (no forced scope refresh); FM-20 (18 vs 3) — rate limiting is REQUIRED only when gxp:true, so non-GxP retains L=3 D=2 (optional rate limiting and monitoring). FM-27 (8 vs 4) — policy input schema validation is enforced only when gxp:true.

All 31 failure modes have been mitigated to RPN < 10. The fifteen failure modes with pre-mitigation RPN >= 20 (FM-03: 20, FM-04: 24, FM-07: 30, FM-08: 20, FM-09: 24, FM-15: 20, FM-16: 24, FM-17: 20, FM-19: 24, FM-20: 27, FM-25: 30, FM-26: 20, FM-28: 36, FM-29: 30, FM-30: 24) have been reduced to residual RPNs of 5, 8, 5, 5, 8, 5, 8, 5, 8, 3, 5, 5, 8, 5, and 4 respectively. FM-28 (stale resource attribute), the highest pre-mitigation RPN at 36, is mitigated to RPN 8 by resource attribute freshness thresholds with configurable `maxAgeMs`, deny on stale attributes, and WARNING logs for missing provenance timestamps (REQ-GUARD-071). FM-29 (expired signing certificate) is mitigated from RPN 30 to RPN 5 by automated certificate lifecycle management with 90/30/7-day renewal thresholds, CRL/OCSP revocation checking, and certificate chain archival (REQ-GUARD-068). FM-30 (algorithm migration chain break) is mitigated from RPN 24 to RPN 4 by epoch-based algorithm transitions with multi-algorithm verification in verifyAuditChain() (REQ-GUARD-069). FM-07 (key exposure), previously the highest post-mitigation risk at RPN 10, has been reduced to RPN 5 by elevating HSM/keystore/secrets manager from RECOMMENDED to REQUIRED when `gxp: true` (ADR #43), bringing Detectability from 2 to 1 (HSM provides automatic tamper detection). FM-25 (unauthorized admin access), with pre-mitigation RPN 30, is mitigated to RPN 5 by AdminGuardConfig deny-by-default enforcement, `checkGxPReadiness()` admin-authority verification, separation of duties, and administrative event logging (section 64g). FM-15 (crash window) is mitigated from RPN 20 to RPN 5 by WAL crash recovery, real-time WAL orphan alerting (structured health check alert on orphaned intent detection when `gxp: true`), and completeness cross-check (per-port resolution vs audit write counters), bringing Detectability from 4 to 1 via two independent automated detection paths. FM-17 (buffer-flush window) is mitigated to RPN 5 by the same WAL mechanism plus flush-interval controls and completeness monitoring. FM-18 (IdP unavailability) is mitigated from RPN 16 to RPN 8 by business continuity planning, health monitoring, and OQ-14 failure handling verification. FM-19 (stale scope permissions) is mitigated from RPN 24 to RPN 8 by mandatory `maxScopeLifetimeMs` when `gxp: true` (ADR #45) and `ScopeExpiredError` (ACL013). FM-20 (evaluation flooding) is mitigated from RPN 27 to RPN 3 by mandatory rate limiting (`maxEvaluationsPerSecond` REQUIRED when `gxp: true`), `RateLimitSummaryAuditEntry` for audit trail visibility, and operational monitoring. FM-21 (evaluationId collision) is mitigated from RPN 15 to RPN 5 by CSPRNG-backed UUID v4 generation. FM-22 (cross-region consolidation) is mitigated from RPN 18 to RPN 6 by evaluationId-based deduplication, timestamp secondary sort, and PQ verification. FM-23 (policy change without audit) is mitigated from RPN 16 to RPN 4 by mandatory `PolicyChangeAuditEntry` recording, separation of duties enforcement, and hash chain participation. FM-26 (completeness discrepancy unescalated) is mitigated from RPN 20 to RPN 5 by automated escalation procedure (1-minute alert, dual routing to ops + QA, 1-hour ack SLA, 4-hour investigation SLA, 24-hour incident report). FM-27 (invalid attribute type) is mitigated from RPN 16 to RPN 4 by policy input schema validation at graph construction time (REQ-GUARD-070). FM-31 (missing predicate rule mapping) is mitigated from RPN 16 to RPN 4 by mandatory predicateRuleMapping when gxp:true and checkGxPReadiness item 15 (REQ-GUARD-067). All 31 failure modes are Low (residual RPN < 10).

> **FMEA Scope Note:** This FMEA covers library-level failure modes with Severity >= 4 (Major and Critical). Low-severity operational concerns (Severity 1-3) are documented in Appendix M (15-appendices.md) as deployment guidance rather than formal failure modes. Organizations SHOULD incorporate Appendix M concerns into their site-level risk assessment.

> **Single-Control-Failure Analysis (S=5):** All 15 failure modes with Critical severity (S=5) (of 31 total, including FM-21, FM-25, FM-26, and FM-29) employ multiple independent mitigations. For each, the loss of any single mitigation does not restore the pre-mitigation RPN because at least one other independent control remains effective. For example: FM-03 (silent entry drop) is mitigated by four independent controls (`failOnAuditError`, `Result` return type, ACL008 alerting, completeness monitoring); FM-07 (key exposure) is mitigated by three independent controls (behavioral contract, REQUIRED HSM/keystore/secrets manager when gxp: true per ADR #43, IQ-10 source scanning); FM-17 (buffer-flush) is mitigated by four independent controls (WAL, buffer backpressure, flush-on-shutdown, completeness monitoring); FM-21 (evaluationId collision) is mitigated by three independent controls (CSPRNG UUID v4, per-scope uniqueness verification, adapter-level duplicate detection); FM-25 (unauthorized admin access) is mitigated by four independent controls (AdminGuardConfig deny-by-default, `checkGxPReadiness()` admin-authority verification, separation of duties enforcement, administrative event log recording); FM-26 (completeness discrepancy unescalated) is mitigated by three independent controls (automated 1-minute alert, dual routing to ops + QA, tiered SLA escalation with 1-hour ack / 4-hour investigation / 24-hour incident report). This layered approach satisfies the REQUIREMENT that no single control failure can restore the pre-mitigation RPN for Critical-severity failure modes.

## 68a. STRIDE Threat Model

#### React — Client-Side Integration Threats

The `integrations/react-guard` package provides React components (`<Can>`, `<Cannot>`) and hooks (`useCan`, `usePolicy`, `useSubject`) for client-side authorization UI. These components gate UI rendering only — they do NOT enforce authorization. All authorization enforcement occurs server-side via the guard adapter and policy evaluator.

> **Key principle:** React guard components are **UI convenience gates**, NOT GxP controls. A user bypassing `<Can>` in the browser still faces server-side guard enforcement on every port resolution. Client-side threats are therefore rated Low to Moderate severity because they cannot compromise the server-side authorization boundary.
